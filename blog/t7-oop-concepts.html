Ruby: Variable Scope

October 21, 2014

What kinds of variables does Ruby support? Where are they accessible? When would you use them and what makes them different? What is scope? Scope defines where the variable is accessible. There are total of 4 different variable scopes and 1 called constants. Each of the scopes have a unique way of starting the variables. Global: $ Class: @@ Instance: @ Local: [a-z] or _ Constants: [A-Z] To detect the scope of the variable, you can use the method called defined? x.defined? Just like variable scopes, private and public limits accessibility of variables. Typing private above the methods inside a class will only allow access to its variables if it has been referenced to a method above. LINK: Technotopia
